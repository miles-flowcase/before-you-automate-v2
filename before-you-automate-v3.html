Chapter 1

The AI paradox in
AEC proposals

Proposal teams at engineering and consulting firms are generating more content faster than ever. AI writing tools can spin up a first draft in minutes. Resumes get auto-formatted. Project descriptions are pulled together without the usual two-day scramble.

So why hasn't the win rate moved?

"AI hasn't improved your win rate. It's just helped you lose faster."

Clients evaluating proposals aren't impressed by volume. They're looking for something specific and difficult to fake: evidence that your firm has genuinely done this kind of work before, with people who are credible and available, on projects that are relevant and verifiable.

The problem isn't the writing. It's never been the writing. The problem is that most firms can't quickly surface accurate, structured, trustworthy expertise data — and no AI tool has fixed that. It can only amplify whatever exists in the data layer beneath it.

The firms winning the most competitive bids aren't the ones with the slickest AI output. They're the ones who can prove — specifically, credibly, and instantly — that their people have done this before.

Chapter 2

The real problem is
expertise data

Ask any proposal manager in a large AEC or consulting firm where their resumes live. The answer is almost always the same: everywhere and nowhere at once.

  • SharePoint folders with seven versions of the same CV
  • Word documents emailed back and forth before every bid
  • HR systems that track employment history, not proposal-ready experience
  • Old proposal files with out-of-date project descriptions
  • People's personal drives and email attachments
  • Tribal knowledge — the senior BD leader who just knows who worked on what

This fragmentation is so common it's become invisible. Teams work around it every time. They spend days before a bid deadline hunting for resumes, chasing consultants for updates, reformatting everything to template, and praying nothing's expired.

And when a firm grows through acquisition — which most large AEC firms do — the problem compounds overnight. Suddenly you've inherited five different systems, three CV formats, and expertise scattered across offices that don't yet speak to each other.

The question isn't whether your AI tool is good enough.
It's whether your data is good enough to feed it.

Chapter 3

Three reasons AI proposal
tools underdeliver

AI proposal tools aren't failing because the technology is immature. They're failing because the data they run on isn't ready. Here's how it plays out in practice.

1

Unstructured data generates irrelevant recommendations

When your expertise data lives in unformatted Word docs and free-text fields, AI can't distinguish between a $5M infrastructure project and a $500K feasibility study. It matches keywords, not actual relevance. The output sounds plausible but misses the point.

2

Outdated data creates compliance and credibility risk

A resume in SharePoint from 2021 may show a PE license that expired in 2023. A project description may list a team lead who left the firm. An AI tool will confidently include both in your proposal. Evaluators may catch it. And if they do, the bid is over.

3

No governance means no trust in the output

Even if the AI generates something reasonable, your proposal team has no way to verify it quickly. They spend the same hours they saved on generation double-checking facts and cross-referencing against other systems. The efficiency gain disappears.

You don't need a better AI writing tool. You need better inputs for the one you already have.

The principles that set them apart

WSB's story isn't unique — it's a pattern. The firms pulling ahead share a common playbook:

Treat expertise as infrastructure, not a document management problem.

Invest in a system of record for people and project data.

Standardize before you automate. Clean data first, AI second.

Make proposal data API-ready so future tools can access it cleanly.

Design for integration, not isolation. Work with HR, CRM, and PSA — don't replace them.

Chapter 6 — Interactive

The AI readiness
checklist

Before you invest in any AI proposal tool, run through this checklist. If you can't check most of these boxes, you're not ready for Layer 2 — and any AI investment will underperform.

0 / 13

Check the boxes that apply to your firm.

Your People Data
Can you search for any employee by skill, certification, project type, or client — firm-wide?
Are all CVs stored in a structured, searchable format — not Word docs in folders?
Do you have a standardized skills dictionary consistent across offices and acquired entities?
Are certifications centrally validated with expiration tracking?

Your Project Data
Can you segment past projects by client, service line, region, and contract value?
Are project descriptions standardized and searchable — not buried in old proposals?
Is project data connected to the people who actually delivered it?
Can you produce a compliance matrix showing team coverage against RFP requirements in minutes?
Your Systems
Can AI tools access your expertise data through an API — without someone exporting a spreadsheet first?
Does your expertise data sync with HR, CRM, or PSA systems?
Is there a single governed system of record, or competing versions of truth across the firm?
If you acquired a company tomorrow, could you integrate their people data within weeks — not quarters?

If you checked fewer than half, you have an expertise data problem. No AI tool will fix it for you.
